week 9: molding and casting

Wine bottle holder



This week I wanted to mold and cast a wine bottle holder. I wanted to make a glow-in-the-dark wine bottle holder. I thought about shaping it like one of the "petals" of the crochet I was going to engrave on one of it's walls.

Lessons learnt:

When designing the object, keep in mind the machine's toolpath and size of the millbit you are using.

Bubbles:

Insert the resin from the bottom up

But keep your object upside down (the part more likely to have bubble issues will be more hidden)

powdering surface with talcum powder or corn starch

Resins:

Polyester really stinks until it's cured. Needs a room with very good ventilation. Increasing the amount of catalyst (usually around 2%) can make a significant difference in curing time.

The resin's viscosity is important -- when adding pigments, it can get a little harder than you thing.
